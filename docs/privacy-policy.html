Calendar Freedom — Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 25, 2026

Overview

Calendar Freedom is a macOS application that adds custom video conferencing room types to Apple Calendar. Your privacy is important to us, and this policy explains how the app handles your data.

Data Collection

Calendar Freedom does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. The app has no network access and does not communicate with any external servers.

Data Storage

Your room type configurations (name, URL, and random ID preference) are stored locally on your device using macOS App Groups, shared only between the Calendar Freedom app and its Calendar extension. This data never leaves your device.

Third-Party Services

Calendar Freedom does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services.

Calendar Access

The Calendar extension provides video conferencing room types to Apple Calendar through macOS's built-in virtual conference provider API. The extension does not read, modify, or access your calendar events or any other calendar data.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the changes will be posted on this page with a revised date.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can open an issue at github.com/mechko/calendar-freedom.
